Giraffe

Giraffes are tall, long-necked mammals known for their distinctive appearance and graceful movements.

Physical Characteristics

  • Size: Giraffes are the tallest land animals, with males reaching heights of up to 18 feet and females around 14 feet.
  • Long Neck: Their long necks have the same number of vertebrae as most mammals, but each vertebra is elongated.
  • Coat Pattern: Giraffes have a unique coat pattern with irregular patches of brown on a lighter background.

Behavior and Habitat

  • Habitat: Giraffes are found in various habitats across Africa, including savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands.
  • Herbivores: They are herbivores, primarily feeding on leaves, flowers, and fruits from tall trees.
  • Social Behavior: Giraffes are social animals and often live in loose groups, called towers, consisting of females and their young.

Diet

Giraffes are herbivores and have a specialized diet that mainly consists of leaves from acacia and other trees.

Conservation Status

Giraffes are listed as "Vulnerable" by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) due to habitat loss and other threats.

Interesting Facts

  • Heart and Blood Pressure: Giraffes have a powerful heart and high blood pressure to pump blood to their brain, given their tall necks.
  • Long Tongue: They have a prehensile tongue that can extend up to 18 inches, helping them grasp leaves and strip them from branches.
  • Communication: Giraffes communicate through vocalizations, body language, and neck movements.
