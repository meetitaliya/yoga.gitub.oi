Impala (Aepyceros melampus):

Habitat: Found in savannas and woodlands across eastern and southern Africa.

Physical Characteristics: Impalas are medium-sized antelopes with reddish-brown coats and a distinctive "M" marking on their rear ends. Only males have curved horns, which can reach up to 28 inches (70 cm) in length.

Behavior: They are known for their leaping ability, which helps them escape predators. Impalas live in mixed-sex herds led by a dominant male.

Diet: Impalas are herbivores, primarily feeding on grasses, leaves, and herbs.

Reproduction: Females give birth to a single calf after a gestation period of about 6 to 7 months.

Gemsbok (Oryx gazella):

Habitat: Found in the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. Physical Characteristics: Gemsboks are large antelopes with a striking appearance. They have long, straight horns that can grow up to 33 inches (85 cm) long. Their coats are grayish-brown with white markings on the face and legs.

Behavior: Gemsboks are adapted to desert conditions and can survive long periods without water. They are known for their excellent sense of smell and keen eyesight.

Diet: Their diet consists of tough desert vegetation, including thorny plants.

Reproduction: They give birth to a single calf after a gestation period of around 8 months.

Thomson's Gazelle (Eudorcas thomsonii):

Habitat: Found in grasslands and savannas across eastern Africa.

Physical Characteristics: Thomson's gazelles are small to medium-sized with a tan coat and distinctive black stripe on their sides. Both males and females have short, curved horns.

Behavior: These gazelles are known for their speed and agility, which help them evade predators. They are social animals and often form herds.

Diet: Their diet mainly consists of grasses.

Reproduction: Females give birth to a single calf after a gestation period of about 5 to 6 months.

Greater Kudu (Tragelaphus strepsiceros):

Habitat: Found in woodlands and savannas of eastern and southern Africa.

Behavior: They are typically solitary or found in small groups. Males use their impressive horns for both defense and attracting mates.
